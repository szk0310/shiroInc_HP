Nagano will enchant you
all year-round

Nagano is one of the nation’s most renowned and remarkable prefectures. Boasting world-class ski fields, captivating snow monkeys, serene onsens, ancient temples, rich cultural heritage, and more, Nagano offers a tapestry of experiences that will enchant you year-round.

Obasute Rice Terraces

The beautiful terraced rice fields of Obasute in Nagano, which has been selected as one of the 100 best terraced rice fields in Japan. Selected as a “Traced Rice Terrace Heritage Certified Area” that connects Chikuma City and Zenkojidaira in Nagano City, it boasts outstanding beauty even in Japan.

Zenkoji Temple

Zenkoji Temple has a history of about 1,400 years since its foundation, with the principal image of the Ikko-Sanson Amida Nyorai. Especially in the Edo period (1603-1867), it is said that so many people visited Zenkoji that it was called “once in a lifetime visit to Zenkoji”. The temple is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Shinshu, with more than 7 million visitors a year. (Photo courtesy of Zenkoji Temple)

Snow Monkey Park

Snow Monkey Park (Jigokudani Yaen Park) is famous for the Japanese macaques that can be seen bathing in Onsen. They are nicknamed “snow monkeys” by people from all over the world.

Chikuma River

The Chikuma River is the longest river in Japan at 367 km in length. From Club Wyndham Chikumakan Nagano, it takes only a few minutes on foot to reach the banks of the Chikuma River, where you can easily enjoy walking and cycling. Every year in August, the Fireworks Festival is held, and 10,000 fireworks are shot off from the banks of the Chikuma River, beautifully coloring the night sky in summer.

Arato Castle Ruins

Arato Castle was built in the 16th century by the Yamada clan, a powerful local clan that ruled this area. The castle was later developed into a historic site park in 1995, and visitors can feel the atmosphere of a mountain castle of that time. Located on top of a mountain about 10 minutes by car from the property, the castle commands a panoramic view. It was used as a filming location for some historical drama.

Photo gallery

Choraku-ji Temple is close to Obasute Rice Terraces, this temple is home to haiku monuments by writers and artists, including Matsuo Basho and Kobayashi Issa. (in Chikuma City)

The apricot blossom blooms all over the village, attracting 100,000 tourists every year during the Apricot Festival in April. (in Chikuma City)

Apricots are a speciality of Chikuma City, harvested from June to July and processed into jam and syrup. (in Chikuma City)

Chikuma River Summer Fireworks Festival: 10,000 fireworks are launched from the banks of the Chikuma River on 7 August every year. (in Chikuma City)

The Zenkoji Daihongan is a branch temple of the Zenkoji in Nagano City, where the public exhibition of religious objects is held once every seven years. (in Chikuma City)

Obasute Station: Located halfway up a mountain at an altitude of 551 m, the scenery from the platform is said to be one of the most beautiful train windows in Japan. (in Chikuma City)

Snow monkeys can be seen soaking in Onsen in winter at Jigokudani Yaen Park, which attracts many tourists from abroad. (in Yamanouchi, Nagano)

Ueda Castle Ruins Park was built by Masayuki Sanada in 1583. It is now maintained as a park and open to the public. (Ueda City, Nagano)
